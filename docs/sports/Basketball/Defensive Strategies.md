## Emphasize Footwork and Leverage

* Fronting the Post (Even Shorter): If guarding a larger player in the post, don't try to push them. Front them aggressively. Your lower CoG gives you an advantage in staying low and denying the passing angle. If the pass goes over you, you rotate for the help-side block-out.
* The 'Pester' Method on the Perimeter: You can't reach over them, so focus on denial and distraction. Use exceptionally quick lateral footwork to deny the first step and force them to use a move they don't want to. Your hands should be constantly active, but focused on the passing lanes or tapping the ball low, not reaching high.
* Low Stance for Box-Outs: When shot goes up, drop your CoG as low as possible. You might not be able to jump over the bigger player, but you can leverage your lower position to get under their hips and seal them off from the basket, forcing them to fight through your low mass for the rebound.
