## Ankles

### Offensive Strategies (Emphasize Angles and Separation)

* The Low-Post Dive/Under-Screen: When screening, don't just stand there. Set a solid screen and immediately cut hard into the paint, staying below the hip of your defender. Because you are lower, this is a very difficult pass for them to block. A quick pass leads to a layup, before the defensive rotation can recover.
* The Micro-Hesitation: When driving, use an extremely quick, subtle hesitation dribble just outside the defender's reach. Your defender, expecting the dominant player's strategy, is likely bracing for a full-contact drive. Your quick, low crossover or spin past the point of contact utilizes your lower center of gravity (CoG) for superior angular momentum, leaving them flat-footed.
* The Jump-Stop & Pivot Mastery: This is your injury prevention and scoring tool. Instead of a long stride-stop (which loads one knee/ankle), utilize a two-foot jump-stop on the catch or end of the drive.
    * Injury Mitigation: Landing on two feet provides a wider base and distributes the force, protecting your ankles.
    * Scoring: It allows you to immediately pivot, shielding the ball with your body and creating space for a short fadeaway or hook shot (which is hard to block because of your low release point).

### Avoid Risky Jumping

* Avoid High-Risk Jump Landings: Land on two feet whenever possible (as suggested with the Jump-Stop). If you jump for a rebound, focus on bringing your feet underneath you on the way down, not reaching out to the sides, which increases the likelihood of landing on someone's foot.
