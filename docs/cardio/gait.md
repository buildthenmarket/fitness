# Gait

## Assessing Gait Imbalance

If you feel an imbalance in your gait, it's important to assess the issue properly. You can start by observing your walking pattern and noting any specific difficulties, such as dragging your feet, feeling off balance, or experiencing irregular steps 

A healthcare provider can conduct a neurological examination to assess movement, sensation, coordination, and balance  This may include performance testing to evaluate your gait and balance more thoroughly 

Additional diagnostic tools might include MRI or CT scans to check your brain and spinal cord for any abnormalities that could be contributing to your gait and balance issues  Blood tests can also be performed to evaluate for underlying causes 

Physical therapy is often recommended to address gait and balance problems, involving exercises to improve standing, walking, and overall movement  Orthotic devices or braces may also be used to support joints and correct conditions of the foot or ankle 

If you suspect you have a gait or balance problem, it is advisable to consult a healthcare provider for a proper assessment and tailored treatment plan
